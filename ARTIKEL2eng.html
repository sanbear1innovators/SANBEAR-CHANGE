The Dangers of Artificial Intelligence (AI) for Environmental Sustainability

How large an energy footprint does technology that makes our lives easier leave behind?


re you a loyal AI user? You're not alone! In this modern era of rapid technological development, the use of AI has significantly simplified human life. Data from Bloomberg Technoz states that 2 out of 3 people worldwide use social media. This translates to approximately 1 billion people globally accessing AI daily. However, behind the convenience offered, there are unseen impacts on the environment. One of these impacts is high electricity consumption.

The high electricity consumption of AI causes many environmental problems. This is a problem for the environment because many power plants still use fossil fuels. This fuel produces carbon emissions that cause climate change. AI requires high energy to answer human questions and the more complex the questions, the more electricity consumption will also increase. MIT Technology Review states that to produce a standard image with 1024x1024 pixels requires 1,141 joules of GPU energy and an estimated total of 2,282 joules. In addition, projections from Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory state that by 2028 half of data center electricity will be used by AI.
